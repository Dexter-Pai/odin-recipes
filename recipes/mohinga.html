Mohinga Soup (Myanmar)



Description

Mohinga (Burmese: မုန့်ဟင်းခါး; MLCTS: mun.hang: hka:, IPA: [mo̰ʊɰ̃hɪ́ɰ̃ɡá]; also spelt mont hin gar) is a rice noodle and fish soup from Myanmar and an essential part of Burmese cuisine, considered by many to be the national dish of Myanmar.[1][2][3] Mohinga is readily available in most parts of the country, sold by street hawkers and roadside stalls in larger cities. Mohinga is traditionally eaten for breakfast.

It provides a good source of Fiber, Vitamins, Minerals, Anti-Inflammatory and Anti-Oxidant properties.



Ingredients

  • 2 quarts fish stock
  • 2 pounds whitefish cut into 1 to 2-inch pieces
  • 2 TBSPs coconut oil
  • 1 large onion or 2 small-medium onions, diced
  • 4-6 garlic cloves diced
  • 3-4 inch piece ginger peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks lemongrass top half (the thinner, green part) removed, bottom half cut into chunks
  • 2 tsps turmeric
  • 1 tsps roasted chile powder
  • 1 cup chickpea flour (also called “fava” flour)
  • Rice noodles cooked according to package directions


Steps

  1. Cut the lemongrass stalks in half. Discard the upper green halves. Take the lower halves chop off the end, and peel away a few of the tough outer layers. Dice the stalk into 1 inch pieces.
  2. Dice the onion, garlic and ginger. Use a food processor, if needed. Heat coconut oil in a medium-sized stockpot over medium heat. Add the lemongrass, onion, garlic, and ginger and saute until fragrant and softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add turmeric and chili powder. Mix well and saute another minute or two.
  4. Add fish broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add chickpea flour and stir until mixed well. Simmer for 10 to 15 more minutes until slightly thickened.
  6. Add the fish. Simmer for about 5 minutes or until it's cooked through.
  7. Prepare noodles according to package directions.
  8. Add rice noodles to individual bowls. Ladle soup over the noodles. Add seasonings, to taste.
